About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Community Health at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. I work with Dr. Chung-Yi Chiu in the area of digital health interventions and human-computer interaction for health. My research focuses on designing patient portals and mHealth solutions that support digital health-seeking behavior, self-efficacy, and sustained engagement.

I have developed digital platforms and conducted implementation research projects in Bangladesh. Now I contribute to multiple NIH-funded projects on AI-supported health tools. Before this, I completed my bachelor in Dentistry and was a licensed dentist in Bangladesh.

Enhancing Cervical & Breast Cancer Screening in Bangladesh

This project introduced an eco-friendly bamboo-based toolkit for VIA and CBE. An 8-day training program was organized for healthcare providers in Khulna and Satkhira Maternal and Child Welfare Centres. Providers were trained on performing VIA and CBE, patient counseling, peer motivation, and digital data management.

Mentorship was provided through hybrid sessions with master trainers, and infrastructural support helped re-establish screening centres that had paused during COVID-19. The intervention received positive feedback and has strong potential for nationwide scale-up.

Friendship 3-Tier Health Program Evaluation

Friendship‚Äôs health program addresses gaps in primary and secondary care in remote char and coastal areas using a three-tier approach:

  • Tier 1: Community Health Workers provide basic preventive services, health education, and referrals at the household level.
  • Tier 2: Satellite clinics and trained paramedics deliver primary healthcare and maternal, newborn, and child health services.
  • Tier 3: Floating Hospitals and referral centers ensure access to specialized medical care and surgical services.

Our evaluation examined service coverage, patient satisfaction, referral efficiency, and health outcomes. The program demonstrated improvements in immunization uptake, antenatal and postnatal care, and early detection of diseases.

Findings highlighted the importance of community engagement, integrated referral pathways, and telemedicine support for sustainability.

Mukhorito: Adolescent SRHR mHealth Platform

Mukhorito is an mHealth intervention addressing the lack of accessible, adolescent-friendly SRHR information in Bangladesh. The app was developed using a co-design process with adolescents, educators, and health experts, ensuring relevance and cultural sensitivity.

Guided by the Intervention Mapping framework and the Health Action Process Approach, Mukhorito integrates evidence-based behavior change strategies with engaging digital features:

  • Interactive learning modules on SRHR, gender, and relationships
  • Peer discussion forums and Q&A with experts
  • Gamified quizzes and challenges
  • Anonymous, stigma-free access to resources

A pilot evaluation showed significant improvements in SRHR knowledge and awareness. Collaboration with the Bangladesh government and private investors supported sustainability and scale-up.
